                 Unit Weapon Selection Note:
  When a unit reference profile mentions OR in the weapons list, a choice between the listed
  options and the following selection must be made before the start of the battle. Any
  associated cost will be noted in the unit's notes section.
  Some weapon options may modify a single value of either a Close Combat (CC) or Firefight
  (FF) stat. In these instances, a (bracket) modifier may be used in place of the unit's stat if
  desired. However, if the weapon changes both values, it will be reflected as a new statline
  and both must be used. When determining what Close Combat or Firefight value to use in an
  engagement with multiple weapon selection options, you may select the best combination of
  values from among the options you have chosen.

                       Alternate Rule: Strategic Value
Some units are better at taking and holding objectives than others, which is reflected in their strategic value. It is often not enough
that you just inflict carnage on your opponent, you have to take and hold ground, too!

 In order to claim objectives, you must have an unbroken formation within 15cm of the objective and it must have a strategic value of
at least 1. If an opponent has an unbroken formation within 15cm of an objective at the same time, then it may attempt to contest
the objective. To contest an objective, the enemy formation must have a Strategic Value of at least 1/2 of the unit attempting to
claim the objective.

Unless specifically noted, unit upgrades do not add to the strategic value of a formation (ex. Predator tanks added to a legion tactical
detachment through the Tank upgrade would not add to the formations strategic value, but a unit of 6 predator tanks from the Iron
Hands legion lArmy List, which is a Line Detachment, would be worth 24SV) .

      Pitched Battles:
      The standard battles are more than adequate to provide a wide variety of experiences without creating specific conditions, and thus
      represents a balanced meeting engagement. However, more pitched battles come in a number of flavours, and these represent a
      number of additional more scenario oriented random missions still based on the tournament rules but with an additional impetus to
      achieve certain select goals without venturing into fully unique scenarios.

      Attacker and Defender:
      Attackers and defenders represent a larger unseen strategic goal dictated to the forces present which will have to achieve tactical
      victories in order to further the greater strategic whole. In order to determine who is the Attacker, and who is the defender, before
      any side is deployed, each player or team should roll a D6 and add their faction’s Strategy rating and compare to see who has scored
      higher. If a tie is rolled, reroll until there is a winner. The winner decides who shall be the attacker, and who shall be the defender.

      Set up:
      Setting up a pitched battle is done in the same way as a standard game: the attacker roll for the mission is done after placing
      objectives (often, available forces will have to make do with strategic command’s greater objectives and adapt to them).

      Victory Conditions:
      The 5 standard tournament goals (Blitzkrieg, Break and Their Spirits, Defend the Flag, Take and Hold, and They Shall Not Pass [see
      page. 12]) still apply, and play a pivotal part in a pitched battle. However, these missions weight the relative value of the standard
      victory goals and influence who will be counted as the victor by awarding certain victory conditions with one or more Victory Points
      (VP). At the end of turn 3, the combatants will check their victory conditions against the 5 standard goals. You must still achieve at
      least 2 of the 5 objectives at the end of the turn, and also score more VP than your opponent. If there is no winner, a fourth battle
      round is played. If there is still no clear winner at the end of turn 4, the battle is a draw.
      Combat Sector: They Shall not Pass occasionally includes an addendum called a Combat Sector. These Combat Sectors are areas of
      the battlefield typically the responsibility of individual detachments on a static front, but for our purposes, divide the battlefield into
      60cm (approximately 24”) sections oriented along the long battlefield edge and extend them straight back across your battlefield
      half. If the They Shall Not Pass is achieved, you can score 1 VP for each Combat Sector so long as you also have 1 unbroken friendly
      unit in the Combat Sector area.
